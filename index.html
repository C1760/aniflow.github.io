Abstract

Static flowcharts can be tedious to follow, especially when they include complex branches or loops.

We present AniFlow, an interactive system for creating animated flowcharts with engaging narration by recommending narrative order and corresponding voiceover scripts. Through a structure-aware graph traversal method, we model flowchart narrative ordering as a sequence of chains, en- abling high-level manipulation of animation logic beyond individ- ual elements and links. This chain-based narrative order serves as a unified interface where visual and audio tracks can be easily aligned and edited. Leveraging large language models, AniFlow supports auto-completion of scripts, helping users to produce high- quality voiceover narration with minimal effort.

We demonstrate the versatility of our approach across diverse scenarios, ranging from food recipes to research pipelines in academic publications. A user study shows that AniFlow reduces workload and effort in cre- ating narrative flowchart animations, while outperforming baseline methods.

Pipeline

Given a user-provided or created flowchart, AniFlow automatically detects the narrative orders among elements and links, and organizes them into a high-level chain structure, supplemented by LLM-generated narrative scripts. The visual and audio tracks are then integrated into a chain-based editing interface that facilitates easy animation modification.
